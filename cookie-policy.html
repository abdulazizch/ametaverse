COOKIE COLLECTION STATEMENT
Our website uses cookies and to disclose that we have formed and written this cookie policy. This cookie policy is written according to the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA – U.S regulation) and the GDPR and UK-GDPR(General Data Protection Regulation – European and UK’s regulation) and we are letting our customers know that their data may be collected and they can contact us to if they want to have their data removed.

Our website collects information through cookies. A cookie is a log file that collects certain information and is stored on the user's or visitor’s device or personal computer. This information will be provided back to the website if such a user or visitor makes a return visit. Our website uses cookies so it can identify and help to track the users of our website. There are many types of cookies, however, most commonly only two types of cookies are used. Session cookies are temporary cookies that are deleted once the user exits the browser while the permanent cookies remain in the users’ or visitor’s browser until the cookie log files are either deleted by the user or expired on their own. Some cookies enable our eCommerce system to remember your order through the checkout process even if you want to check out as a guest user. Mainly we use cookies in order to (i) understand how visitors are arriving on our website; (ii) in order to monitor the usage of our website; (iii) remember when a user last visited our website and if the cookie persists continuing that user’s usage from the last time; (iv) in order to keep tracking we may store analytical cookies; (v) track for the purpose of marketing; (vi) for error reporting; (vii) for customer services; and (viii) to conduct research and development by gathering user data in aggregated form. A functional cookie is a type of permanent cookie that is necessary for the purpose of some services. Our website stores these cookies for our services in connection with third-party integration with our website.

Please note that the cookie does not contain any personal identifying information. Users can also change their browser settings to make that the cookies are not being collected by making sure the collection of the cookies is disabled. However, doing so will make many of our services ineffectual.

BLOCKING AND DELETING OUR WEBSITE COOKIES
Most browsers allow you to refuse to accept cookies. For example: In Internet Explorer you can refuse all cookies by clicking “Tools”, “Internet Options”, “Privacy”, and selecting “Block all cookies” using the sliding selector;

In Firefox you can block all cookies by clicking "Tools", "Options", and un-checking "Accept cookies from sites" in the "Privacy" box.

In the Chrome web browser after you have opened Chrome, at the top right, click more and then settings. Under "Privacy and security," click Site settings. Click Cookies and from there, you can: Turn on cookies: Next to "Blocked," turn on the switch.

Please remember that cookies are files created by sites you visit. They make your online experience easier by saving browsing information. With cookies, sites can:
• Remember your site preferences.
• Give you locally relevant content.

Thus, blocking all cookies will have a negative impact upon the usability of our website. If you block cookies, you will not be able to use several features on this website.
